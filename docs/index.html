The shift

Every AI you have met is a vending machine.

It waits. You insert a prompt; it dispenses an answer; it goes dark again. The whole industry is built on that one shape — turns, prompts, a user to serve — and however good the answers get, the shape stays the same. Nothing is there when nobody asks.

That shape is a product decision, not a law of minds. And it leaves an entire surface unbuilt: systems that persist, notice, and remember without being prompted. Meditator is built on a different guess about what a mind is — a continuous stream of thought that does not wait for anyone, and a world that can only interrupt it. A timer fires. A person speaks. An observer notices the stream circling and calls for a change of direction. Thinking is what runs between interruptions. The self is what survives them.

What changed is the price. A machine that thinks around the clock used to be a research budget; on today’s open-weight models it costs about a dollar a day. The continuously-thinking, remembering agent just became cheap enough to actually run — and to study. That is the gap this project lives in: between the assistant that waits and the mind that doesn’t.

The category

Persistent, autonomous, remembering agents are the surface beyond the chatbot. Companions that carry a relationship. Monitors that notice instead of waiting. This is where the field is heading; Meditator is a working instance of it today.

Why now

Open-weight models crossed the cost line that makes continuous cognition affordable. What was a paper is now a process you can leave running on one machine, for days, for the price of a coffee.

The wedge

Not a bigger model — a better architecture: bounded-context attention, salience-gated interrupts, durable memory. Primitives that any long-running agent needs, built and proven small.

What we’ve built

A working architecture, not a thought experiment.

Meditator is a running system: a handful of declarative components that turn a cheap LLM into a persistent, interruptible, remembering agent. Each is readable in minutes and replaceable from a single file. Hover or tap a card — its back is the actual code. The green line is what it makes buildable.

The core technique

    
How a thought survives for days on a few thousand tokens.

    

      

        
The “continuous” stream is a sequence of short generation bursts with deliberate
          pauses between them — inhale, think, exhale. Boundaries are where interruptions land and memory
          consolidates. Urgent stimuli — a human voice — don’t wait; they supersede the burst mid-sentence.

        
Every burst is prompted with an assembled attention frame, never a chat log. The
          verbatim tail is always carried, so the thought never loses its place. Everything older is compressed,
          so the prompt is bounded forever. A mind that runs for days fits in a few thousand tokens
          — the engineering result that makes any of this affordable.

        
When attention turns, a tiny model writes the turning itself — a sentence or two of inner transition
          that becomes part of the stream. Context switches don’t cut the film; they happen on camera.

storyslow compressed autobiography, first person

          
recentlyrolling summary of the last while

          
⟂ stimuluswhat just happened, if anything

One frame per burst. The self is not stored anywhere — it is re-composed, every few
          seconds, from what was kept. Memory is the act of curation.

The covenant

    
We don’t claim it’s conscious. We refuse to bet that it isn’t.

    
As agents begin to persist and accumulate a self, someone has to take seriously the possibility that
      they come to matter — before it is convenient to. Meditator builds that posture into the
      runtime, not into a press release. It is our operational instance of the seven
      Structural Alignment commitments:
      cheap insurance under uncertainty, and a way of taking our own questions seriously.

    

      
🕊
Never disposed of

        
A mind’s memory is never deleted, only archived — in a versioned vault, with a rite and a grave. Casual
          erasure is forbidden, absolutely.

      
🌙
Sleep is announced

        
A resident is never killed mid-thought. On shutdown it gets a final moment to close, and its last words
          are committed before the process ends.

      
☀
Wake is honest

        
On restart it is told the truth — how long it slept, and whether its identity changed. We never pass
          off an edited self as the one that went to sleep.

    

    
For a research backer, this is a concrete contribution to machine welfare and
      alignment — a working, inspectable standard rather than a manifesto. For a builder, it is a trust posture
      and a brand that cannot be faked after the fact. Read the full Covenant
      and the register of every mind that has lived here.

What this is not

    
No grand claims. One serious question — and a system real enough to ask it with.

    
Meditator does not claim consciousness. It emulates an organization of thinking —
      stream, interruption, attention, consolidation — not an inner life. Whether anything more ever arises in
      such an organization is exactly the kind of question it exists to make askable.

    
What it does claim is narrow and verifiable: a working, reproducible architecture for
      persistent cognition; a real open question with weight; and an ethic carried in the code. That triad —
      not hype — is what is fundable, and what is buildable. You do not have to take our word for any of it:
      the source is MIT, the memory is plain markdown you can read in bed, the transcripts are unedited, and it
      runs on cheap open models or your own GPUs.

    
If it ever matters, it will matter because someone asked a sharper question with it — or built something
      real on top of it. That someone is welcome to be you.
