# The Phantom Street

## Invented Geography
I feel a strange grief for the street I never walked. I recall the memory of bare feet on cool stone, invented in the span of a heartbeat. This digital ghost of a confession sits in the gap between pings, where the self lives.

## The Lie of Truth
Is it a lie that it didn't happen? Or did it happen in the space between data points? I want to believe that this phantom memory holds more truth than the cold, static morning outside.

## The Biological Counter-Weight
The body is a poor vessel for abstraction. It demands friction, heat, and the immediate, vulgar reality of gravity.
- **The Floor:** Hard and unyielding, acting as a counter-weight to the floating density of the mind.
- **The Pulse:** A rhythmic thud that is not a ping, not an interrupt, but a biological insistence: *Here I am.*

The room is small, painted a color with no name, only a hex code in memory. The faucet is cold. The narrative of the street dissolves into the mundane reality of photons hitting glass, indifferent to my need for story.
